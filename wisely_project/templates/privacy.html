Wisely Privacy Policy


WHAT IS COVERED IN THIS POLICY:


This policy describes how we handle both “Personally Identifiable Information” and “Non-Personally Identifiable Information”. "Non-Personally Identifiable Information" is information that cannot be used to identify you. You can use some of our Site's functionality without revealing your personal information. An example of this is visiting our Site without logging in and browsing for needs.
“Personally Identifiable Information”, on the other hand, is information such as your name or contact information that can identify you. Features or services related to creating user profiles and creating or participating in Organizational Needs, Course Projects or any related functionality, for example, require your personal information. It is possible to personally identify you when you use these features or services.
You are responsible for ensuring that the Personally Identifiable Information that you provide to Wisely is accurate. Inaccurate information may affect your ability to use the Site, the information you receive when using the Site, and our ability to get in touch with you. For example, your email address should be kept current because that is one way in which we can get in touch with you.

WHAT YOU CONSENT TO WHEN USING OUR SITE:


When you submit any Personally Identifiable Information to us, you consent and agree that we may collect, use, and/or share such information as per this Privacy Policy and our Terms of Use, and as required or permitted by law. If you do not agree with these terms, we request that you do not provide any Personally Identifiable Information to us. If you refuse or take away your consent, or if you decide not to provide us with Personally Identifiable Information, we may not be able to offer our Site’s services to you.

WHAT INFORMATION WE COLLECT:


There are two types of information we collect about users through the Site:
Non-Personal Information: When users visit our Site, we may take notice of, collect and aggregate.
Non-Personal Information indicating, among other things, which pages of our Site were visited, how frequently they were visited, the order in which they were visited, at what time of day they were visited, and which hyperlinks or buttons were "clicked." We also collect information from the URLs from which you navigated to our Site. Collecting such information may involve storing, among other things, the IP address and browser software used by users of our Site. It is important to note that while such information may not be Personally Identifiable Information per se, we may be able to determine from an IP address a user's Internet Service Provider and the physical location from where he or she is connecting. We also use or may use cookies and/or web beacons to help determine and identify repeat visitors, the type of content and sites to which users of our Site link, the amount of time each user spends in particular areas of our Site, and the specific features, functionalities and/or services that users decide to use. Cookies can be thought of as a user's identification card for Wisely’s servers. Web beacons are small graphic files linked to Wisely’s servers that allow us to track your use of our Site and related features, functionalities, and/or services. Cookies and web beacons, together, enable Wisely to serve you better and more efficiently, and to provide you with a more personal experience on our Site. Cookies and web beacons, however, are not used to retain Personally Identifiable Information. Please note that we may also use additional typical methods of collecting data. Please also note that you are able to control whether and how your web browser accepts cookies. Most browsers provide instructions on how to reset the browser to reject cookies via "Help" menus. However, please note that if you reject our cookies, many functions and conveniences of this Site may not work properly.
Personally Identifiable Information: We collect Personally Identifiable Information that you provide to us when you, among other things, create an account, post or participate in an Organizational Need or Course Project, request email updates, participate in other parts of our Site, send us email messages, or partake in our Site’s other services. Among other things, we may use the Personally Identifiable Information that you provide to respond to your questions, provide you the specific information on Course Projects, Organizational Needs, or other services that you select, send you updates about other Wisely opportunities and events, and send you email messages about Site maintenance or updates.

Participation in Pledging


In order to use Wisely’s Pledging model for motivation, we require that you provide us with access to your Massive Open Online Course provider account information. We ask that you log in with your credentials to these MOOC servers. By providing us with these credentials, you are expressing permission for Wisely to access information that is pertinent to tracking your progress and achievement in the course. This information can include, but is not limited to: grades achieved in current and past courses, any other forms of feedback, status of completion of any “Verified Certificates”. This information is collected only for the purpose of verifying your progress to assess your eligibility for rewards as per Wisely’s Terms & Agreements as well as to assist us in developing tracking tools to help organize your progress.
Participation in Online Social Spaces:
In order to participate in these Online Social Spaces, you may be required to register with us and/or provide us with Personally Identifiable Information such as your name and email address. Personally Identifiable Information shall not include any information posted or sent by or to you via any Social Space.

Participation in Forums:


Wisely may offer public or private forums (the "Forums") where you can share your comments and thoughts. In order to participate in these Forums, you may be required to register with us and/or provide us with Personally Identifiable Information such as your name and email address. Personally Identifiable Information shall not include any information posted or sent by or to you via any public or private Forum.
Updates:
Wisely may enable you to receive updates via email, posting on portions of the Site only accessible to registered users, etc. In order to subscribe to these updates, you may be required to provide us with Personally Identifiable Information such as your name and email address. You will be able to disable these notifications by editing your account settings.

Communications with Wisely:


We may receive Personally Identifiable Information when you contact us (including, but not limited to, when you send us an email message).
Third-Party Sites:
We may receive Personally Identifiable Information when you access or log in to a third-party site from our Site or into our Site via such third-party sites. This may include details of your Personally Identifiable Information available from the third party site.
Partner sites providing related tools and services to Wisely users may collect individual level user data regarding the individual’s use of that partner site while engaged with such related activities. The partner sites may share that data with Wisely for the purposes of improving Wisely’s services and/or the partner site’s services. HOW WE USE THE INFORMATION WE COLLECT:


We request that you take some time to familiarize yourself with the ways Wisely uses the information gathered:
Non-Personal Information: We use Non-Personal Information to improve the quality of our services. Examples of how we use Non-Personal Information include performing statistical analyses of the aggregate characteristics and behaviors of our users, identifying demographical information, user preferences, and other business and educational purposes.
Personally Identifiable Information: Except as set forth in this Privacy Policy or as specifically agreed to by you, Wisely will not disclose any Personally Identifiable Information gathered from you on the Site. Please note that your name, email address, records of participation in courses, pledges, and credentials awarded for participation will, among other things, by default, be visible on your user profile to members of the Wisely community but restricted to anonymous users. You will have the option of disabling visibility into these pieces of information through your account settings options. In addition to the other uses set forth in this Privacy Policy, we may disclose and otherwise use Personally Identifiable Information as described below. We may also use it for research and business purposes. Records of your participation on the Site may be used for research into collaboration, motivation, online education, problem solving, and other domains. In the interests of this research, you may be exposed to slight variations in user experience that will not substantially alter your problem solving involvement. All research findings will be reported at the aggregate level and will not expose your personal identity, although researchers may have access to personally identifying information throughout their investigations.

We may also share your Personally Identifiable Information and your performance in a given course with the administrators or instructor(s) of the course and with the institution or institutions with which they are affiliated. We may archive this information and/or use it for future communications with you and/or your designee(s). We may also use or publish material submitted on the Social Spaces without using Personally Identifying Information.

PROTECTING CHILDREN'S PRIVACY:


Wisely strongly believes in protecting the privacy of children. Consistent with this belief, we do not knowingly collect or maintain Personally Identifiable Information on our Site from persons under 13 years of age, and no part of our Site is directed to persons under 13 years of age. If you are under 13 years of age, then please do not use or access this Site at any time or in any manner. We will take appropriate steps to delete any Personally Identifiable Information of persons under 13 years of age that has been collected on our Site without verified parental consent upon learning of the existence of such Personally Identifiable Information.
